When to use a passive
When the subject of the verb, is more important than the person or thing carrying it out, the object.
The object of the passive form is usually left out (not important) or preceeded by ‘by.



In general : The object in the active sentence becomes the subject of the passive sentence. : The passive always contains a form of the auxiliary verb ‘to be and a past participle form of a verb.
